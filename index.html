How every PGI score is built

The same three steps drive both the State index (PGI 2.0) and the District index (PGI-D) — only the totals, domains and band-setting differ.
1

Score each indicator

Each indicator earns proportionate value × its weight. The optimum/benchmark for every indicator is fixed in advance.

e.g. Maths, Grade 3 (weight 30): if 50% of children are proficient → 30 × 0.50 = 15 points.
2

Sum to domain & total

Indicator scores add up to domain scores; domains add up to the overall total — 1000 for States, 600 for Districts.

UP State: LO 81.4 + A 52.7 + I&F 73.4 + E 231.2 + GP 68.6 + TE&T 61.8 = 569.14.
3

Place into a grade band

The total is mapped to one of 10 grade bands, from Utkarsh (top) to Akanshi-3 (lowest). Districts/States are graded, not ranked.

569.14 falls in 521–580 → grade Akanshi-1.

Worked examples

Tracing the arithmetic end-to-end for the State and three contrasting districts.
State — Uttar Pradesh
Add the six domain scores: 81.4 (Learning Outcomes) + 52.7 (Access) + 73.4 (Infrastructure) + 231.2 (Equity) + 68.6 (Governance) + 61.8 (Teacher Edu.) = 569.14 / 1000.  The State bands place 521–580 in Akanshi-1. UP sits 11 points below the Prachesta-3 threshold (581) — closing the Learning-Outcomes gap is the fastest route to the next band.

Note on reverse-scored indicators. A few indicators score in reverse — for example, teachers assigned non-teaching duties: a district with fewer such assignments earns more points. Likewise, Equity "gap" indicators reward a smaller gap between social categories. The dashboard's "% of weight" already reflects these direction conventions.
